Unlike FHA loans, conventional loans are not backed by a government agency. They follow guidelines set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. That means slightly stricter credit and income requirements — but also more flexibility on property types and no upfront mortgage insurance premium.

Common Questions

What Buyers Ask About Conventional Loans

What credit score do I need?
Most conventional loans require a minimum credit score of 620, though 680 or higher gets you meaningfully better rates. The difference between a 680 and a 740 score can be worth tens of thousands of dollars over the life of the loan — it's worth knowing where you stand before you apply.
How does PMI work and when does it go away?
Private mortgage insurance (PMI) is required when you put less than 20% down. It protects the lender — not you. The good news: once you reach 20% equity in the home, you can request that PMI be removed. It automatically cancels at 22% equity based on your original purchase price.
What's the loan limit for conventional loans in California?
In most California counties, the 2025 conforming loan limit is $806,500 for a single-family home. Orange County has a higher limit. Loans above this threshold become jumbo loans with different requirements. I'll tell you exactly which category your target home falls into.
Should I get a conventional or FHA loan?
If your credit score is above 680 and you have at least 5–10% to put down, conventional is usually better long-term — lower total cost and no upfront MIP. If your credit score is lower or your savings are limited, FHA may be the better path. I'll run both for your exact numbers before you decide.
Can I use a conventional loan for investment properties?
Yes. Conventional loans can be used for primary residences, second homes, and investment properties. Down payment requirements and rates differ based on property type — investment properties typically require at least 15–25% down.
