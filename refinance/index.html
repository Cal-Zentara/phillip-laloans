Types of Refinance

Which Refinance Makes Sense?

01
Rate & Term
Replace your current loan with a new one at a lower rate or different term. Reduces your monthly payment or helps you pay off sooner. The most common refinance.
02
Cash-Out Refinance
Borrow against the equity you've built. Get cash for home improvements, debt payoff, or other needs — while replacing your existing mortgage with a new one.
03
FHA Streamline
A simplified refinance for existing FHA loan holders. Less paperwork, no appraisal required in most cases. If you have an FHA loan, this may be the fastest path to a lower rate.
04
VA IRRRL
Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan — for veterans with an existing VA loan. Minimal documentation, no appraisal, lower rate. Fast and straightforward if you qualify.
Common Questions

What Homeowners Ask Me

How do I know if refinancing makes sense?
The standard rule is that refinancing makes sense if you can lower your rate by at least 0.5–1%, and you plan to stay in the home long enough to recover the closing costs. I'll calculate your break-even point so you know exactly when the savings kick in — and whether it's worth it for your situation.
What does a refinance cost?
Closing costs typically run 2–5% of the loan amount. Some lenders offer no-closing-cost refinances, but those costs are usually rolled into a slightly higher rate. I'll show you both options so you can choose what fits your timeline and goals.
How long does a refinance take?
Usually 30–45 days from application to close. Streamline refinances (FHA or VA) can move faster because they require less documentation and no appraisal in most cases.
Will refinancing hurt my credit score?
There will be a small, temporary dip from the credit inquiry — typically 5 points or less. If refinancing saves you money long-term, that short-term impact is not a meaningful concern.
Can I refinance if I don't have much equity?
It depends on how much equity you have and what type of loan you're refinancing into. Some programs have more flexibility than others. Call me with your current loan balance and home value and I'll tell you what options are available.
