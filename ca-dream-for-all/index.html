What It Is

Down Payment Covered by the State

Maximum Down Payment Coverage
Up to 20%

The California Housing Finance Agency (CalHFA) provides up to 20% of your home purchase price as a shared appreciation loan — meaning you don't pay it back monthly. Instead, when you sell or refinance, you repay the original amount plus a share of the home's appreciation.

This program is not a grant — it's a deferred loan from the state. You don't make monthly payments on it. When you eventually sell the home or refinance, you repay the original assistance plus a percentage of how much the home has increased in value. For many buyers, this tradeoff is well worth it — especially if the alternative is waiting years to save a larger down payment in a market where prices keep rising.

Who Qualifies

Do You Qualify?

Do I have to be a first-time buyer?
Yes. The CA Dream For All program is specifically for first-time homebuyers — defined by CalHFA as someone who has not owned a primary residence in the past three years. This opens the door to many people who bought previously but have been renting for a few years.
Are there income limits?
Yes. Income limits vary by county and household size. California is expensive, so the limits are generally reasonable — many buyers in Orange County and the surrounding area fall within range. The exact number depends on your specific situation, which is why a quick call is the fastest way to find out.
Does it apply to any type of home?
The program has property type and price limit restrictions. It works with primary residences — single-family homes, condos, and certain manufactured homes within CalHFA's loan limits. I'll tell you upfront whether the specific property you're looking at qualifies.
Do I still need a down payment of my own?
The program covers up to 20%, but you'll typically still need a small down payment of your own (as low as 3–5% depending on the loan type). Combined with the state assistance, this dramatically lowers your total out-of-pocket cost to buy.
Is this program still available?
CalHFA opens and closes the program based on funding availability. When it's open, eligible buyers should move quickly — funding is allocated on a first-come, first-served basis. Call me at 714-726-6333 to find out the current status and whether you can get in line.
