## Design Context

### Users
California homebuyers, refinancers, and property investors. Bilingual English/Vietnamese. Often first-time buyers who feel anxious — buying a home is the biggest decision of their life. They've likely been quoted by 2-3 other lenders and are trying to decide who to trust.

### Brand Personality
**Honest. Premium. Patient.**

Phil's edge is that he won't push the easiest loan to close — he'll find the right one for the situation. The site should feel like a quiet conversation with someone who knows what they're doing, not a sales pitch.

**Emotional goal:** the visitor leaves feeling **confident and in control** — like they finally understand the process and have a guide who's on their side.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Dark — charcoal `#141414` background with Loan Factory orange `#E8622A` as the single accent color. **Do not change the palette.** The charcoal-over-navy decision was deliberate (navy + orange read sporty/athletic; charcoal keeps it premium).

**Typography:** Cormorant Garamond for headings/italic accents + Outfit for body and buttons. Editorial display + clean modern body. Don't swap these.

**Reference tone (not visual copy):** the calm authority of private bank / boutique wealth advisor sites — JPMorgan Private Bank, Bessemer Trust, Sotheby's Realty. Quiet, slow, lots of room to breathe. Editorial.

**Anti-references:**
- Cheesy Loan Factory marketing assets (the "when they go high" ad, stock infographics)
- Generic lender sites with smiling-couple stock photos
- Sporty/athletic combos (the original navy + orange direction)
- Anything that screams "AI slop": glassmorphism, gradient text, side-stripe borders, identical card grids, hero-metric templates

### Design Principles

1. **Quiet authority over loud salesmanship.** No urgent CTAs, no countdown timers, no "Get a quote in 30 seconds!" energy. The site should make the visitor lean in, not back away.
2. **Editorial spacing.** Generous whitespace, varied rhythm, not the same padding everywhere. Treat it like a magazine spread, not a lead-gen funnel.
3. **One accent color, used rarely.** Orange earns its impact by being scarce — the 10% in the 60-30-10 rule. Don't sprinkle it everywhere.
4. **Patient hierarchy.** Big idea first, then the proof, then the path forward. No competing CTAs in the same viewport.
5. **Bilingual respect.** Phil serves Vietnamese-speaking families. Any future copy or section should hold up in both languages — no idioms or wordplay that breaks in translation.
