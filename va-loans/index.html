VA loans allow eligible veterans and active-duty military to purchase a home with zero down payment. There is no private mortgage insurance (PMI), which saves hundreds of dollars every single month compared to conventional and FHA loans. This is a benefit you've already earned — let's use it right.

The VA loan program is backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. It doesn't mean the VA lends you money — it means they guarantee a portion of the loan, which allows lenders to offer you better terms than you'd get on a standard mortgage. The result is a genuinely competitive loan with fewer upfront costs and lower monthly payments.

Do You Qualify?

Who Can Use a VA Loan?

Who is eligible for a VA loan?
Veterans, active-duty service members, National Guard members, Reserve members, and surviving spouses of veterans who died in the line of duty. Generally, you need 90 consecutive days of active service during wartime, or 181 days during peacetime. Call me and I'll verify your eligibility in one conversation.
What is a Certificate of Eligibility (COE)?
The COE is a document from the VA that proves you're eligible to use your VA loan benefit. I can request it on your behalf — you don't need to track it down yourself. It's one of the first steps we handle together.
Is there a VA funding fee?
Yes. The VA charges a one-time funding fee instead of monthly PMI. The amount depends on your down payment and whether you've used a VA loan before. Some veterans — including those with service-connected disabilities — are exempt entirely. I'll tell you upfront what your fee will be.
What types of properties qualify?
Single-family homes, condos in VA-approved developments, and multi-unit properties up to 4 units (if you live in one). The home must be your primary residence — VA loans are not for investment properties.
Can I use a VA loan more than once?
Yes. VA loan entitlement can be restored once you pay off or sell the previous home. In some cases you can even have two VA loans at the same time. I'll explain exactly where you stand based on your history.
How does a VA loan compare to a conventional loan?
For most eligible veterans, VA loans win on nearly every metric — lower rates, no down payment, no PMI. The only case where conventional might make more sense is if you have 20% down and exceptional credit. We'll run both options side by side so you can see the real numbers.
