Your Options

Loans Built for First-Time Buyers

There is no single "first-time buyer loan." There are several programs — each with different down payment requirements, credit score minimums, and income limits. The right one depends on your specific situation. Here's the overview.

01
FHA Loan
As low as 3.5% down. Flexible credit requirements starting at 580. The most common path for first-time buyers in California with limited savings.
02
CA Dream For All
California's state program covers up to 20% of your down payment as a deferred loan. No monthly payments on the assistance — repaid when you sell or refinance.
03
Conventional 97
Only 3% down for qualifying buyers with good credit. No upfront mortgage insurance premium. A strong option if your credit score is above 680.
04
VA Loan
Zero down and no PMI for eligible veterans and active military. If you've served, this is likely your best option — period.
The State Program

CA Dream For All —
Most Buyers Don't Know It Exists

State Down Payment Assistance
Up to 20%

California's Dream For All program covers up to 20% of your purchase price as a shared appreciation loan. You don't make monthly payments on it. For many first-time buyers, this turns homeownership from a 5-year goal into something possible right now.

Most loan officers don't mention this program because it adds steps to the process. I mention it because for qualifying buyers, it can eliminate the biggest obstacle — saving a large enough down payment while California home prices keep rising.

Common Questions

What First-Time Buyers Ask Me

What counts as a "first-time buyer"?
Most programs define it as someone who hasn't owned a primary residence in the past three years. So if you owned a home before but have been renting for a few years, you may qualify as a first-time buyer again. This opens the door for a lot of people who don't realize they're eligible.
How much do I actually need to buy a home?
It depends on the loan type. FHA starts at 3.5% down. Conventional can be as low as 3%. With CA Dream For All, you may only need 3–5% of your own money and the state covers the rest of the down payment. I'll give you an exact number based on the home price you're targeting.
What credit score do I need?
FHA loans accept scores as low as 580 with 3.5% down, or as low as 500 with 10% down. Conventional loans generally require 620 or higher. The higher your score, the better your rate — but you don't need perfect credit to buy a home.
How long does the process take?
From application to close typically takes 30–45 days. Getting pre-approved takes 1–3 business days after you submit your documents. Pre-approval is the first step — it tells you what you can afford and shows sellers you're serious.
Should I talk to a loan officer before I find a home?
Yes — always. Getting pre-approved before you start house hunting tells you your real budget, prevents you from falling in love with a home you can't afford, and puts you in a much stronger position when you make an offer. Call me before you start looking.
