The Basics

What Is an FHA Loan?

Minimum Down Payment
3.5%

An FHA loan is a mortgage backed by the Federal Housing Administration. Because the government insures the loan, lenders can offer lower down payments and approve buyers with less-than-perfect credit — making it the most accessible path to homeownership for first-time buyers in California.

FHA loans aren't just for buyers who can't get a conventional loan — they're often the smartest choice for first-time buyers who haven't built up a large down payment yet. Keeping more cash on hand after closing gives you a financial cushion for repairs, furniture, and life.

If you're putting down less than 20%, you'll pay mortgage insurance (MIP) on an FHA loan. That's a monthly cost on top of your payment, and it's worth understanding before you commit. I'll show you the full picture so you can decide whether FHA or conventional makes more sense for your numbers.

Common Questions

What Buyers Usually Ask

Can I use an FHA loan if I've owned a home before?
Yes. FHA loans are not limited to first-time buyers. If you've owned before but sold and are buying again — or you previously lost a home to foreclosure but meet the waiting period — FHA may still be an option. Call me and we'll look at your specific history.
What's mortgage insurance and do I have to pay it forever?
FHA loans require two types of mortgage insurance: an upfront premium (1.75% of the loan, typically rolled into your loan amount) and an annual premium paid monthly. For most FHA loans today, the monthly MIP stays for the life of the loan — which is one reason some buyers switch to a conventional loan once they've built equity and can refinance.
Are there loan limits on FHA loans in California?
Yes. FHA loan limits vary by county. In high-cost areas like Orange County, Los Angeles, and the Bay Area, the limits are set higher to reflect local home prices. For 2024, the FHA limit in Orange County is $1,149,825 for a single-family home. I'll tell you exactly what the limit is for the county you're buying in.
How does FHA compare to conventional?
FHA: lower credit score minimum, lower down payment, easier to qualify — but has monthly MIP for the life of the loan. Conventional: higher credit score required, more down payment flexibility, mortgage insurance drops off once you hit 20% equity. The right choice depends on your credit, savings, and how long you plan to hold the loan. I'll model both and show you the real numbers.
Can I combine an FHA loan with CA Dream For All?
Yes — and this is one of the most powerful combinations for California first-time buyers. The CA Dream For All program covers up to 20% of your down payment and can be paired with an FHA first mortgage. That means your out-of-pocket down payment drops dramatically. Call me to find out if you qualify for both.
How long does an FHA loan take to close?
Typically 21–45 days from application to closing, depending on how quickly documents come together and how busy the market is. I stay on top of every file and communicate where things stand so nothing stalls without you knowing why.
